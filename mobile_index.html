Top Revenue Driver
Skincare leads revenue, contributing ~40% of total sales. Prioritise replenishment and supplier reliability for this category above all others.
✅
Best Quality Supplier
Supplier 1 manages the largest SKU volume with competitive defect rates. SKU14 (0.10%) and SKU67 (0.13%) are quality benchmarks to replicate.
🚢
Optimal Transport
Sea freight offers the best cost-to-time ratio. Air transport averages 2× cost — restrict to high-value, urgent SKUs only.
⚠️
Quality Risk
38% of SKUs failed or have pending inspections. SKU42 leads with a 4.94% defect rate. Immediate audits needed for Supplier 5 in Bangalore & Kolkata.
📍
City Underperformance
Chennai shows above-average defect rates relative to its production volume. Quality control investment lags behind Mumbai and Bangalore peers.
💡
Pricing Anomaly
High-price SKUs ($80–$99) are underperforming on units sold. SKU84 ($89) sold only 618 units. A modest price reduction could unlock significant volume.
🔄
Route Efficiency
Route B consistently achieves lower total logistics costs. Consolidating onto Route B could reduce logistics spend by an estimated 12–18%.
📈
Scale Opportunity
Significant economies of scale appear above 700 production volume units. SKUs below 300 volume are 35–40% more expensive per unit to produce.
📦
Stock Alert
12 SKUs have stock levels below 10 units — including high-revenue earners. Immediate replenishment review recommended.
